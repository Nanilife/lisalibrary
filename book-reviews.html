Book Reviews

Read student reviews and discover new books recommended by the LISA community.

Persepolis

Author: Marjane Satrapi

Persepolis is a masterpiece as it not only delves deeper into Iran's history and culture, but also presents the tragedies of the country in a mixture of melancholic and humorous way. The short comic reliefs and harsh contradictions are two of the most interesting elements of this graphic novel that make it absolutely absorbing! I especially loved the presentation of Satrapi's childhood!

Reviewed by: Sharonya Banerjee, 7M

How I Live Now

Author: Meg Rosoff

Meg Rosoff's How I Live Now follows Daisy, an American adolescent sent to England just before war breaks out. Living with her cousins in the countryside, she encounters love, fear and endurance as civilization crumbles around them. Daisy evolves from a fearful, self-centered girl to one that is brave, kind and sympathetic.

I admired How I Live Now for its emotional depth, realistic characters, and intense atmosphere. What I enjoyed most was how immersive the story felt and it really gave you a deeper understanding of how hard it was to experience life during war.

Reviewed by: Debora Ciobanasu, 4L

Chasing Cezanne

Author: Peter Mayle

Chasing Cezanne by Peter Mayle is a humorous thriller set in Southern France. The story follows Andre Kelly, a photographer who becomes involved in a mysterious art theft connected to a priceless Cezanne painting. As the mystery unfolds, the book mixes adventure, romance, and humor in a way that keeps the reader interested.

What I liked most was how immersive the story felt. Peter Mayle describes the settings and characters so vividly that it almost feels like you are right there beside Andre, experiencing everything with him. The plot was unique and unpredictable, which made it hard to stop reading.

Reviewed by: Sarah Ciobanasu, 4L

The Hunger Games

Author: Suzanne Collins

A thrilling story that dives deep into the themes of survival, inequality, power, fear and control. It takes place in the post-apocalyptic country called Panem, once known as North America. The story follows the challenges the main protagonist Katniss Everdeen has to go through during the Hunger Games.

I personally find the book amazing, even though sometimes it is kind of hard to keep up with. It is a mix of emotion and suspense, leaving you pleased and willing to read the whole trilogy. The character description is a 10/10 even compared to the movies. It is a must read!

Reviewed by: Raya Penova, 4M

The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes

Author: Suzanne Collins

Suzanne Collins strips away the safety of a hero. The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes follows a young Coriolanus Snow, desperate, brilliant, and dangerously ambitious, as he mentors Lucy Gray Baird, a District tribute in the 10th Hunger Games.

Previously a black-and-white villain, future President Snow emerges here as something far more uncomfortable: ambiguous, even sympathetic. What I most enjoyed was witnessing the spectacle of the Games offset by quiet moments that unravel Snow’s morality until you finally see a resemblance between his current self and future self.

Reviewed by: Emma Mesini, 7L
